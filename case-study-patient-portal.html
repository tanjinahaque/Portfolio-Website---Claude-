Accessibility Case Study

Patient Portal Redesign

An embedded accessibility partnership for a healthcare portal redesign, focused on appointment management, secure messages, test results, and billing workflows.

Role
Accessibility Analyst
Timeline
Discovery to Launch
Scope
Responsive Portal
Standards
WCAG 2.1 AA

Overview

The healthcare team wanted accessibility built into the redesign instead of audited only at the end. I worked with product, design, engineering, and QA to define accessibility requirements, review patterns, test prototypes, and validate production-ready flows before launch.

Challenge

Patient portals contain sensitive, high-stakes information. People need to understand appointments, medication instructions, lab results, and billing actions without confusion or assistive technology barriers.

  • Clinical information needed clear headings, landmarks, and reading order.
  • Message threads and alerts needed accessible status communication.
  • Complex tables for results and billing needed meaningful structure.
  • Mobile layouts needed to preserve labels, instructions, and action clarity.

Process

  • Reviewed wireframes and prototypes for navigation, hierarchy, touch targets, and focus flow.
  • Defined accessibility acceptance criteria for core components before development.
  • Tested interactive prototypes and coded builds with keyboard, VoiceOver, NVDA, and zoom.
  • Partnered with designers on contrast, typography, form states, and error messaging.
  • Created QA checklists so accessibility verification became part of release readiness.

Key Findings

  • Some alert banners were visually prominent but not announced to screen readers.
  • Lab result tables needed stronger header relationships and responsive behavior.
  • Several icon-only actions needed accessible names and clearer visible context.
  • Focus states needed stronger contrast across the design system.

Impact

  • 0 critical accessibility issues remained at launch.
  • 100% color contrast pass rate for audited components.
  • Full ARIA landmark structure implemented across key templates.

Reflection

This project showed the value of accessibility analysis during design, not just after development. By shaping components and acceptance criteria early, the team avoided expensive rework and launched with a more reliable experience for patients.
