Designing Accessible Focus States

Focus states show keyboard users where they are. A strong focus system should be visible, consistent, and designed with the same care as hover and active states.

Focus Must Be Easy to See

WCAG 2.2 strengthened expectations around focus appearance. Teams should design focus indicators that stand out from adjacent colors, remain visible at different zoom levels, and are not clipped by containers.

  • Use a clear outline, ring, underline, or background change that is not color alone.
  • Check focus against light and dark surfaces.
  • Avoid removing browser focus styles without replacing them.

Real Example: Card Components

If a whole card is clickable, keyboard focus should make the active card obvious. A subtle shadow alone may not be enough. A visible outline or ring helps users confirm which card will open before pressing Enter.

Focus States Belong in the Design System

Buttons, links, inputs, chips, tabs, menus, and cards should all have documented focus behavior. This prevents teams from solving the same accessibility problem differently in every feature.
