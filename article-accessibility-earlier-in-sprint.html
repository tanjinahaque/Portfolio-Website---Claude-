Product Workflows

Finding Accessibility Issues Earlier in the Sprint

Accessibility work is most effective when teams identify barriers before they become release blockers. Early review helps product, design, development, and QA move with more confidence.

Move Accessibility Into Planning

Accessibility should be part of the conversation when requirements are shaped. Product owners can reduce risk by asking what assistive technology behavior, keyboard support, error handling, and content structure will be expected before work enters development.

Review Design Before Build

Design reviews can catch many issues before code exists. Focus states, touch targets, contrast, labels, hierarchy, and interaction notes are easier to improve when the team is still shaping the experience.

  • Confirm visible focus states for interactive elements.
  • Document labels, instructions, and error states in the design file.
  • Identify custom components that need keyboard or screen reader behavior.

Give QA Clear Acceptance Criteria

When accessibility expectations are included in acceptance criteria, QA can validate behavior earlier and developers can fix issues before they become harder to unwind.
